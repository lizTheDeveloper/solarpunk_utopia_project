# Economic Alternatives and Solidarity Movements Specification

## Overview

This specification covers alternative economic models, worker cooperatives, solidarity economy, community investment, and connection to broader social movements. These systems build economic democracy, solidarity networks, and movements for systemic transformation.

## Alternative Economic Models

### REQ-ECON-001: Resource-Based Accounting
The platform SHALL support resource-based accounting that tracks material flows and needs rather than monetary exchange.

**Rationale**: Money abstracts away actual resource use and needs; tracking real resources enables post-scarcity planning.

#### Scenario: Community resource inventory
- **GIVEN** community wants to understand resource abundance
- **WHEN** tracking resources
- **THEN** the system SHALL:
  - Inventory actual physical resources (food, tools, materials, skills)
  - Track flows and transformations
  - Identify abundance and scarcity
  - Plan based on real needs, not money
  - Make resource visibility accessible
  - Build post-monetary economic thinking

### REQ-ECON-002: Contribution Visibility
The platform SHALL make contributions visible and celebrated without creating debt, obligation, or hierarchy.

**Rationale**: Recognizing contributions builds participation and gratitude; measurement without obligation maintains gift economy.

#### Scenario: Contribution celebration
- **GIVEN** community members contribute variously
- **WHEN** viewing contribution patterns
- **THEN** the system SHALL:
  - Visualize diverse contribution types (labor, resources, creativity, care)
  - Celebrate abundance and participation
  - Avoid creating debt or obligation
  - NOT rank or compare competitively
  - Build culture of gratitude not accounting
  - Maintain gift economy principles

### REQ-ECON-003: Time-Based Mutual Credit
The platform SHALL support mutual credit systems based on labor time, distinct from time banking.

**Rationale**: Mutual credit enables exchange without external money; trust-based systems build economic autonomy.
#### Scenario: Mutual credit circle
- **GIVEN** community wants mutual credit system
- **WHEN** trading within credit network
- **THEN** the system SHALL:
  - Enable credit creation through labor exchange
  - Track positive and negative balances
  - Maintain zero-sum balance across network
  - Set reasonable credit limits
  - Build trust-based exchange network
  - Reduce dependency on external money

### REQ-ECON-004: Gift Economy Principles
The platform SHALL embody and teach gift economy principles of giving freely, receiving graciously, and circulating abundance.

**Rationale**: Gift economy creates stronger bonds than market exchange; teaching these principles builds post-capitalist culture.

#### Scenario: Gift economy education
- **GIVEN** community wants to deepen gift practice
- **WHEN** learning together
- **THEN** the system SHALL:
  - Teach gift economy anthropology and theory
  - Practice giving without expectation
  - Receive graciously and gratefully
  - Circulate gifts rather than hoarding
  - Build gift culture consciously
  - Transform economic relationships
